Why should I Learn to Code?
  • Learning to code needs critical thinking and encourages inquisitiveness. Kids learning to code at an early age are preparing for the future. Coding helps develop communication, creativity, mathematical and writing skills as well as building confidence.
  • The computational thinking involved in coding genuinely contributes to improved reading comprehension.
  • Kids exposed to new languages at a young age develop stronger verbal and written skills. Programming languages help children to be better communicators and train logical thinking abilities.
  • In the relatively near future not having basic coding skills will be tantamount to not knowing how to read. It is a basic literacy requirement. Most modern roles require basic IT skills with, for example, retail and fast food positions requiring competence with tech and computers.
  • Learning to deal with 'failure is important. Learning to be more resilient and to try again while learning from your mistakes is an intrinsic part of the coding experience and teaches young kids the importance of trying again until you succeed.
Why should I Learn to Code?
  • With more than 54% of professional coders writing their first line of code in their mid teens and coding now a crucial component of a well rounded education. Gaining these skills during school age is now mandatory especially as mainstream education hasn't yet met the demand for these skills.
  • Anything that builds confidence and a positive self-image is important. Coding demonstrates and teaches that any issue is fixable, any obstacle can be conquered through repeated persistence and teamwork. Programming cultivates the perfect environment for collaborative working.
  • The future is a digital one that will be structured on a technology based foundation. If today's teens don't have a solid understanding of this future then they will effectively be preventing themselves from gaining access to opportunities.
  • Extracurricular programming studies provide additional skill-sets that boost higher education portfolios and are arguably more essential for a fully rounded education than before.
  • The experience gained from learning programming skills in the classroom prepares students for real-world situations and prepares them for a strong progression into the work environment.
Why should I Learn to Code?
  • Fact: Only around 5% of software developers worldwide are women. But it’s not a permanent situation. Things are changing for the better and if you’re seriously considering a career in computing you may be thinking “Is coding a good career choice for me?”
  • Why go for a job as a developer? It’s a brilliant job! Software developers roles are regularly some of the best salaried. It’s a fast growing industry. Coding jobs in the U.S. alone are predicted to increase by over 22% within the next decade.. There are over quarter of a million positions needing skilled coders right now!
  • Depending on the role it isn't mandatory to have a Computer Science degree to be employed as a software developer. A high percentage of software developers consider themselves self taught or have come into the sector through alternative educational paths.
  • It’s easier to gain relevant skills without having to obtain an expensive and time consuming degree, there are many varied options for self learning and supported courses, including studying online and boot camps amongst the most prominent.
  • Although the gender bias within the industry is still a big topic of concern it is improving every day and there are many ways to kick off a coding career. Software developers are employed by all types of companies both large and small. A high percentage of coders work remotely, so there are always healthy alternatives to purely office based roles.
